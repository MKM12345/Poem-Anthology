Implication poem

I love English

Oh I love English so much,
Love it so much,
Yes, so very much,
Love it, Love it, Love it,
I love it so much.

How the teacher drone on & on,
Loquaciously talking about their son,
Yes, I find it so interesting,
The vast amount of piling homework,
Turning my eyes red,

How my attention wavers,
I look out the window,
Slowly scanning the area,
I see a bird, oooh! Pretty!
And notice every feature for the next 20 minutes,

Oh yes, I find it interesting,
So very much,
I love it so much,
I feel like my heads gonna explode.
And my insides gonna cook inside.

The stacked-up buildings of paper,
Oh very good, very good, indeed,
I look at the everlasting tower,
My eyes straining to see the top,
I’m gonna need to buy a flamethrower soon.

“Oh”, the teacher says,
“Come back for extra revision at lunch”,
“Cool”, I mutter,
I’d love to come,
But I have urgent business in the football pitch,
So I have to rush!
Maybe next time”.

Protest Poem

Grr...Pen Ink.

Why does my pen ink run out so quickly?
I write an essay with and it runs out,
Why is the quality so sickly?
Even when I write a small story route!

The refills my poor dad has to buy,
It cost him a fortune!
Even if it’s a parker pen- oh my!
For just a few notes and homework in my room

The top is dented for uncapping the pen again and again,
The boring motions is the norm,
Even if the window shows a bored hen,
I can’t even finish a form,

The vast amount of energy use,
Just because the pen can’t write.
Even if my mum offers her award-winning moose,
It does nothing to my pounding fright.

So what do we stand for:
GIVE GOOD PENS!
What do we stand for:
KICK FAT HENS!
What do we stand for:
MAKE IT LAST LONGER!
What do we want it for:
TO MAKE US STRONGER!

Diatribe

THEODORE

Theodore, treacherous traitor, eating all my italian pizza.
Me and my mamma missions, making me turn a blind eye.
To your diabolical deliverences, rampaging raids, pathetic paces,
TO EAT EVERYTHING I OWN!
You even took a bite of the murderous mushrooms!
I had nothing for Princess Peach,
Even if I checked my award-winning warehouse,
Where only the tattered remains of burnt crust and vegetables lay,
Oh, not even my old age will stop me from revenge, even in my old honda!
I was starving for weeks,
chowing on leaves,
eating whatever I could,
My business almost collapsing!
Now even though I'm older, frailer, weaker,
I will chomp and guzzle your desserts,
Eat your treats,
Devour your cakes,
To see how you like to starve!
Boisterous Boy,
Pathetic Pooter,
Dilapidated dozer,
Touching Turner,
Wait till I come for vengeanence,
Oh ho ho, mamma mia!

Music poem

Dead ends

At the dawn of night,
A mournful sight,
Remembering dead friends,
The soldiers sit around a campfire, sitting in dead ends.

But the camp gets attacked and maimed,
Muskets fire to the warriors of famed,
Tanks come to give a hand,
As they make their last stand.

Now they are all dead.
Hounds of the enemy fed,
But there is a glimmer of hope,
As reinforcements come down the slope.

A massive phalanx of troops,
Their presence makes the enemy swoop,
Yelling war cries and banging of drums,
The army kills the enemy in a chant of hums.

As they leave no one alive,
They march in one massive bee hive,
They go to celebrate,
Rejoicing the enemies fate,

First they dance slowly,
Keeping their gaze lowly,
Then they get animated,
Their dances fated.

A mix of happy and sad sound,
Their break finally found,
As they remember their dead friends,
This time, though, in no dead ends.

Sensory Poem

Percy Pigs

The sweet smell wafted up my nose,
As the percy pig stayed for a pose.
Worse then my brothers cavities, the smell,
Sent my skull reverberating like a bell.

I gently smushed it in my palm,
It felt like sheep's wool in a farm,
A cloud so high in the sky,
The joy there - oh my.

I looked - a pause in time.
I can't think of a suitable rhyme.
A look of simple simplicity,
Not a speck of any hidden atrocity,

Ah, yes! The time I have been waiting for,
As soon as I put one in my mouth, I wanted more,
The juicy, strwberry taste, flooded my glands,
I even started licking my hands!

Like a gummy bear, rubbery and so nice,
Even sweeter then sweet-chicken rice!
Filling light in the hall of dark,
The delicious taste left a profound mark.
